## The business problem

Every business runs on a stream of events — orders, payments, clicks, shipments, sensor
readings. The problem is that a raw event is thin. An order says *customer 4821 bought
SKU 12 for $89*. To actually **do** something the moment it happens — greet a
high-value customer differently, flag a risky transaction, promise an accurate delivery
date, make a personalized offer — you need to fuse that event with **live context** that
lives somewhere else: the customer's loyalty tier and lifetime value, current inventory,
a risk score, a model's prediction.

Doing that enrichment *in real time* has traditionally meant building and operating a
**stream-processing application**: stand up consumers, call out to internal services,
manage state and retries, handle schema changes, scale it, keep it up at 3 a.m. Every new
enrichment is another service to own. The result is that most teams give up and push the
work to a nightly batch job — and the moment to act has already passed.

## What Confluent Cloud changes

On Confluent Cloud the enrichment becomes **declarative**. The events already flow through
Kafka; you describe the enrichment in **Flink SQL**, and Confluent runs it for you — no
application to build, deploy, or operate. The whole path, end to end, is configuration:

- **Get the events in** with a fully-managed **source connector** (a database via CDC, a
  SaaS app, an existing topic) — no connector code.
- **Enrich in flight with Flink SQL.** Join the stream to reference data, and — this is
  the part that removes the most custom code — **look up live data from your own systems
  and services per event**, including calling an internal REST API or running **AI model
  inference** (a hosted LLM, a fraud model) right inside the query.
- **Govern it automatically** with Schema Registry, data contracts, and lineage, so the
  enriched stream is trustworthy and discoverable.
- **Deliver everywhere** with fully-managed **sink connectors** to operational systems,
  and **Tableflow** to expose the enriched stream as open **Apache Iceberg** tables for
  analytics — no ETL.

## A concrete example: orders into decisions

Take an online retailer. Orders land on a topic. The business wants every order, the
instant it arrives, turned into a **personalized action** — enriched with the customer's
loyalty tier and lifetime value from the internal Customer-360 service, then handed to a
language model that writes a tailored offer — and the enriched result delivered to the
marketing platform and the analytics lakehouse.

In the old model that's a bespoke consumer app plus an integration to the Customer-360
service plus an LLM integration plus two delivery pipelines — all to build and run. On
Confluent Cloud it's a handful of SQL statements over managed connectors:

- the order stream is enriched, per event, with a **live call to the Customer-360 API**;
- an **AI model** turns the enriched order into a personalized offer;
- the result flows out through managed sinks and Tableflow.

No stream-processing service is written or operated. And because the enrichment runs
inside Confluent Cloud, sensitive customer data can be reached over **private
networking** — it never has to touch the public internet.

## Why it matters

Real-time enrichment is where streaming data stops being plumbing and starts driving
decisions. Making it **declarative** collapses the time from "we have the events" to "we
act on them" from a quarter-long engineering project to an afternoon of SQL — and removes
the operational tax of yet another service to keep alive. The same pattern powers fraud
scoring, dynamic pricing, logistics ETAs, and customer 360s.
